# Part 1C.1: Creating Your AWS Account

## Goal

In this lesson, you will create your Amazon Web Services (AWS) account and complete the initial registration process.

By the end of this chapter, you will have:

* Prepared the information required to register.
* Created an AWS account.
* Verified your email address.
* Added your contact information.
* Added a payment method.
* Completed telephone verification.
* Selected an AWS Support plan.
* Signed in to AWS for the first time.
* Identified your AWS Account ID.

At this stage, **do not create any AWS resources**. We will first ensure your account is ready and secure.

---

# 1. What You Need Before Creating an AWS Account

Before opening the AWS registration page, prepare the following information.

## 1.1 A Valid Email Address

AWS requires an email address that will become the primary contact for your account.

AWS uses this email address for:

* Account verification
* Security notifications
* Billing notifications
* Budget alerts
* Password recovery
* Important service announcements

Choose an email address that:

* You check regularly.
* You expect to keep for many years.
* Only you can access.

Do **not** use:

* Temporary email addresses
* Shared family email accounts
* Work email addresses that you may lose access to
* School email addresses that may expire

---

## 1.2 A Strong Password

Your password protects the root user of your AWS account.

Create a password that:

* Is long
* Is unique
* Contains uppercase and lowercase letters
* Contains numbers
* Contains special characters

Do not reuse passwords from:

* Email
* GitHub
* Banking
* Social media
* Other websites

You will strengthen your account further with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) in the next chapter.

---

## 1.3 A Mobile Phone

AWS uses telephone verification during account creation.

You should have access to a mobile or landline number capable of receiving the verification request.

---

## 1.4 A Payment Method

AWS requires a valid payment method during registration.

Depending on your location, this is commonly a supported debit or credit card.

Even if you intend to stay within Free Tier limits, a payment method is still required for account verification and for charges that may occur outside Free Tier eligibility or limits.

---

## 1.5 A Stable Internet Connection

Avoid creating your AWS account on an unstable connection.

Interrupted registration can make verification more difficult.

---

## Verification

Before continuing, confirm that you have:

* An email address
* A strong password
* A telephone number
* A supported payment method
* A reliable internet connection

---

# 2. Creating an AWS Account

Open your web browser.

Navigate to the AWS home page and select the option to create a new AWS account.

AWS may periodically update the appearance of its registration pages, but the overall registration process remains similar.

During registration, AWS will ask you to:

1. Enter your email address.
2. Choose an AWS account name.
3. Create a password.
4. Verify your email.
5. Provide contact information.
6. Add a payment method.
7. Complete telephone verification.
8. Select a support plan.

Take your time and read each page carefully.

---

# 3. Choosing an AWS Account Name

AWS asks you to provide an account name.

This is primarily a friendly label to help identify the account.

Examples include:

```text
Ann Personal AWS
AWS Learning Lab
VERIQTA Training
Cloud Projects
```

The account name does not affect:

* Your AWS services
* Resource names
* Billing calculations
* Website addresses

You can change the account name later if necessary.

---

# 4. Creating Your Root Password

The password you create during registration belongs to the **AWS root user**.

The root user has full control over the AWS account.

Choose a password that is:

* Unique
* Difficult to guess
* Never reused elsewhere

Avoid passwords based on:

* Birthdays
* Phone numbers
* Family names
* Dictionary words

Do not write your password inside:

* README files
* HTML files
* Git repositories
* Screenshots
* Notes stored with your project

Use a password manager whenever possible.

---

# 5. Verifying Your Email Address

After submitting your registration details, AWS sends a verification email.

Open your email inbox.

Look for an email from Amazon Web Services.

If you do not see it:

* Check the Spam folder.
* Check the Junk folder.
* Wait a few minutes.
* Request another verification email if necessary.

Follow the instructions in the email to confirm ownership of the address.

Your account registration cannot continue until the email address has been verified.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* You received the verification email.
* You successfully confirmed the email address.
* You can sign in using that email address.

---

# 6. Adding Contact Information

AWS asks for contact information to identify the account owner.

Typical information includes:

* Full name
* Address
* City
* State or province
* Postal code
* Country
* Telephone number

Provide accurate information.

Incorrect information may create problems during:

* Billing
* Identity verification
* Account recovery
* Support requests

---

## Personal vs Business Account

AWS commonly asks whether the account is:

* Personal
* Business

If you are learning AWS for yourself, select **Personal**.

If you are registering on behalf of a company, choose **Business**.

The available AWS services are generally the same. The choice mainly affects account profile information.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* Your name is correct.
* Your address is correct.
* Your telephone number is correct.
* Your country is correct.
* You selected the appropriate account type.

---

# 7. Adding a Payment Method

AWS requests a payment method during account creation.

Follow the instructions on the billing page and enter the required information.

Depending on your country, AWS may perform a temporary authorization to verify the payment method.

The exact amount and handling of this authorization can vary by region and payment provider.

Adding a payment method does **not** mean AWS will automatically charge you for using Free Tier–eligible resources within applicable limits.

However, resources that fall outside Free Tier eligibility or exceed usage limits may generate charges.

Always review current AWS pricing before creating billable resources.

---

## Payment Security

Never:

* Share your card details publicly.
* Include payment information in screenshots.
* Save payment details inside project files.
* Send payment information through unsecured channels.

AWS stores billing information separately from your project files.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* Your payment method was accepted.
* No validation errors remain.
* Registration continues to the next step.

---

# 8. Completing Telephone Verification

AWS performs telephone verification to help confirm account ownership.

Follow the instructions displayed during registration.

Depending on your location, AWS may:

* Send a verification code.
* Call your phone.
* Ask you to enter a code displayed on the registration page.

Complete the verification exactly as instructed.

If verification fails:

* Confirm your phone number.
* Confirm your country code.
* Wait a few minutes before retrying.
* Ensure your phone can receive the verification.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* Telephone verification completed successfully.
* Registration continues without errors.

---

# 9. Selecting an AWS Support Plan

AWS asks you to choose a support plan.

For beginners, the **Basic Support** plan is normally sufficient.

Basic Support provides access to:

* Customer service
* Documentation
* AWS Trusted Advisor core checks
* Personal Health Dashboard
* Service Health Dashboard

More advanced paid plans include additional technical support and response-time options for organizations with greater operational requirements.

You can change your support plan later if your needs change.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* You selected a support plan.
* Registration completed successfully.

---

# 10. Waiting for Account Activation

After registration, AWS prepares your account.

Activation is often completed quickly, but in some cases it may take longer while AWS completes verification.

During this period:

* Keep your verification email.
* Do not create another AWS account because you think registration failed.
* Wait for confirmation from AWS.

Once activation is complete, you can sign in.

---

# 11. Signing In for the First Time

After your account has been activated:

1. Open the AWS sign-in page.
2. Choose **Root user**.
3. Enter the email address used during registration.
4. Enter the password you created.
5. Complete any requested verification.

You are now signing in as the **root user**.

The root user has complete control over the AWS account.

In the next chapter, you will learn why the root user should only be used for limited account-level tasks.

---

## Verification

Confirm that:

* You successfully signed in.
* The AWS Management Console opens.
* No sign-in errors appear.

# 12. Understanding Your AWS Account ID

Every AWS account has a unique **12-digit AWS Account ID**.

Example:

```text
123456789012
```

This number uniquely identifies your AWS account.

It is different from:

* Your email address
* Your AWS account name
* Your password

The Account ID is used in many AWS features, including permissions, cross-account access, and resource identification.

You do not need to memorize it, but you should know where to find it.

Do not confuse the Account ID with sensitive credentials such as passwords or secret access keys.

# 15. Knowledge Review

## Question 1

Why do you need a permanent email address for AWS?

**Answer:** It is used for account verification, security notifications, billing alerts, password recovery, and important AWS communications.

---

## Question 2

Does creating an AWS account automatically make every AWS service free?

**Answer:** No. AWS offers Free Tier benefits for eligible services and usage, but charges can still apply depending on the services, configurations, and usage.

---

## Question 3

Which support plan is generally appropriate for beginners?

**Answer:** Basic Support.

---

## Question 4

What is the AWS Account ID?

**Answer:** A unique 12-digit identifier assigned to your AWS account.

---

## Question 5

Should you begin creating AWS resources immediately after signing in as the root user?

**Answer:** No. Secure the root user first, then create an administrative IAM user before performing everyday work.
