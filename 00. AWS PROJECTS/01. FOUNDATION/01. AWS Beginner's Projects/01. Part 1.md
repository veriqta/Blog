## **Preparation — Your Foundation Before You Build**

Welcome. If you've ever opened the AWS Console and felt overwhelmed by the sheer number of services, buttons, and options, you're not alone. This guide was built specifically to eliminate that confusion. We're going to walk through three real projects, step by step, click by click, command by command. No assumptions. No skipped steps. No "just do this" without showing you exactly where to click.

By the end of this handbook, you will have:
- Built and hosted a static website on Amazon S3 with global CDN delivery via CloudFront
- Completed the famous "Cloud Resume Challenge" — a real portfolio piece
- Launched and configured a live EC2 web server running Apache

Let's begin with preparation. This is the boring-but-critical stuff that will save you from surprise bills and security headaches later.

## **1.1 What You Will Build**

Before touching a single button, know exactly what you're building:

**Project 1: Static Website on S3 + CloudFront**
You'll create a professional-looking static website (HTML/CSS) and host it on Amazon S3. Then you'll put Amazon CloudFront (a Content Delivery Network) in front of it so your site loads blazingly fast from anywhere in the world. You'll learn S3 buckets, bucket policies, static website hosting, and CDN configuration.

**Project 2: Cloud Resume Challenge**
You'll build a personal resume website, deploy it to S3, serve it through CloudFront, and walk through the entire process of planning, structuring, designing, validating, and cleaning up. This isn't just uploading a file — it's a complete project workflow.

**Project 3: EC2 Web Server**
You'll launch a real Linux virtual machine in the cloud (EC2), connect to it via SSH, install and configure the Apache web server, deploy HTML content, and test it from your browser. You'll work with Security Groups, key pairs, and terminal commands.

## **1.4 AWS Account Security — The Right Way to Start**

AWS security is not optional. One mistake — like exposing your root account credentials — can lead to unauthorized access and unexpected charges. We're going to set up your account properly from day one.

### **Creating Your AWS Account (If You Don't Have One)**

**Step 1:** Open your browser and go to: `https://aws.amazon.com`

**Step 2:** Click the orange button in the top-right corner that says **"Create an AWS Account"**

**Step 3:** You'll see a page titled "Sign up for AWS"
- Enter your **Email address**
- Enter a **Password** (make it strong — at least 8 characters, mix of upper, lower, numbers, and symbols)
- Enter an **AWS account name** (this is just a label — use something like `veriqta-learning` or your name)
- Click **"Continue"** (it may say "Verify email address")

**Step 4:** Email Verification
- AWS will send a verification code to your email.
- Open your email inbox, find the email from AWS, and copy the verification code.
- Paste it into the box on the AWS page and click **"Verify"**

**Step 5:** Contact Information
- Select **"Personal"** (unless you're doing this for a company)
- Fill in your full name, phone number, country, and address.
- Check the box agreeing to the AWS Customer Agreement.
- Click **"Continue"**

**Step 6:** Payment Information
- Enter your credit or debit card details.
- AWS will make a small temporary authorization charge (usually $1) to verify your card. This is refunded.
- Click **"Verify and Continue"**

**Step 7:** Identity Verification
- AWS will call or text you with a verification code.
- Select your country code, enter your phone number, choose **"Text message (SMS)"** or **"Voice call"**
- Enter the security check characters (the CAPTCHA)
- Click **"Send SMS"** or **"Call me now"**
- When you receive the code, enter it and click **"Continue"**

**Step 8:** Select a Support Plan
- Choose **"Basic support — Free"**
- Click **"Complete sign up"**

**Step 9:** Welcome to AWS
- Click **"Go to the AWS Management Console"**
- Sign in with your email and the password you just created.

## **1.5 IAM Administrator — Never Use Your Root Account**

Your root account (the email you just signed up with) has god-like powers. If someone steals those credentials, they can do anything — including deleting everything and running up massive bills. The very first thing you do after creating an AWS account is create an administrator user with limited (but still powerful) access, and then **lock away your root account**.

### **Step-by-step: Creating an IAM Administrator User**

**Step 1:** Make sure you're signed into the AWS Console.

**Step 2:** In the top search bar (it says "Search for services, features, blogs, docs, and more"), type: `IAM`
- Click on **"IAM"** (Identity and Access Management) when it appears.

**Step 3:** You'll land on the IAM Dashboard. Look at the left sidebar. Click on **"Users"**

**Step 4:** Click the blue button **"Create user"**

**Step 5:** On the "Specify user details" page:
- Under "User name", type: `admin-veriqta`
- Check the box that says **"Provide user access to the AWS Management Console"**
- Select **"I want to create an IAM user"**
- Under "Console password", select **"Autogenerated password"** (AWS will create a strong password for you)
- **Uncheck** the box that says "Users must create a new password at next sign-in" (we'll manage this ourselves)
- Click **"Next"**

**Step 6:** On the "Set permissions" page:
- Select **"Attach policies directly"**
- In the "Permissions policies" search box, type: `AdministratorAccess`
- Check the box next to **"AdministratorAccess"** (this policy has an orange warning triangle — that's normal, it means it's very powerful)
- Click **"Next"**

**Step 7:** On the "Review and create" page:
- Review the details. The user name should be `admin-veriqta` and the policy should be `AdministratorAccess`.
- Click **"Create user"**

**Step 8:** Download Your Credentials
- AWS will show you a success page with the user's sign-in details.
- **CRITICAL:** Click the **"Download .csv file"** button.
- Save this file as `admin-veriqta-credentials.csv` inside your `VERIQTA-AWS-Projects` folder.
- This file contains the sign-in URL, username, and password. Guard it carefully.
- Click **"Return to users list"**

### **Step-by-step: Enabling MFA on Your Root Account**

**Step 1:** In the IAM Dashboard, look at the right side. You may see security recommendations. Click on **"Add MFA"** or navigate to your root account settings.

Alternatively:
- Click your account name in the top-right corner of the AWS Console.
- Click **"Security credentials"**
- Scroll down to "Multi-factor authentication (MFA)" and click **"Assign MFA device"**

**Step 2:** On the "Manage MFA device" page:
- For "Device name", type: `root-mfa`
- Select **"Authenticator app"** (use Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator on your phone)
- Click **"Next"**

**Step 3:** Set up the authenticator app:
- Open your authenticator app on your phone.
- Tap the "+" or "Add account" button.
- Choose "Scan a QR code" and point your camera at the QR code shown on the AWS screen.
- The app will generate a 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds.
- Enter **two consecutive codes** in the boxes labeled "MFA code 1" and "MFA code 2"
- Click **"Add MFA"**

**Step 4:** Confirmation
- You should see a green success message.
- From now on, when you sign in with your root account, you'll need your password AND the 6-digit code from your phone.

### **Step-by-step: Creating a Sign-in Bookmark for Your IAM User**

Your IAM user does NOT sign in at `aws.amazon.com`. It uses a special URL.

**Step 1:** Go back to the IAM Dashboard.

**Step 2:** On the right side, you'll see a section called **"AWS account"** with an **"IAM users sign-in link"**

**Step 3:** It looks something like: `https://123456789012.signin.aws.amazon.com/console`
- The number is your 12-digit AWS account ID.
- Click **"Copy"** next to this link.
- Save this URL in your bookmarks or notes. Label it: "AWS Console - admin-veriqta"
- **From now on, use this URL to sign in.** Do not use the main AWS sign-in page for daily work.

**Step 4:** Sign out of your root account and test signing in as your IAM user.
- Go to the IAM sign-in link you just copied.
- Enter:
  - Account ID (or account alias): Your 12-digit account ID (or you can set an alias)
  - IAM user name: `admin-veriqta`
  - Password: The one from your downloaded CSV file
- Click **"Sign in"**

You should now be in the AWS Console as your administrator user.

## **1.6 Billing & Free Tier Awareness**

AWS has a generous Free Tier for new accounts, but it's not unlimited. You need to know what costs money and what doesn't.

### **What's Free for 12 Months (from account creation):**
- **EC2:** 750 hours per month of t2.micro or t3.micro (Linux or Windows)
- **S3:** 5 GB of standard storage, 20,000 GET requests, 2,000 PUT requests
- **CloudFront:** 50 GB data transfer out, 2,000,000 HTTP/HTTPS requests per month
- **Data Transfer:** 15 GB out per month

### **What ALWAYS Costs Money (even in Free Tier):**
- **Data transfer OUT** beyond the free limits
- **Elastic IP addresses** not attached to a running instance
- **Some storage classes** in S3
- **CloudFront** if you exceed the limits (but 50 GB is generous)

### **Step-by-step: Setting Up Billing Alerts**

**Step 1:** In the AWS Console search bar, type: `Billing`
- Click on **"Billing and Cost Management"**

**Step 2:** In the left sidebar, click **"Budgets"**

**Step 3:** Click **"Create budget"**

**Step 4:** Choose "Use a template (simplified)"
- Select **"Zero spend budget"** (this alerts you if you spend even $0.01)
- Or select **"Monthly cost budget"** and set an amount like $5 or $10
- Click **"Create budget"**

**Step 5:** Enter your email address for alerts.
- Click **"Create"**

Now you'll get an email if your spending exceeds the threshold.

### **Step-by-step: Enabling Detailed Billing**

**Step 1:** In Billing and Cost Management, click **"Billing preferences"** in the left sidebar.

**Step 2:** Check the box for **"Receive PDF invoice by email"**

**Step 3:** Check the box for **"Receive free tier usage alerts"**

**Step 4:** Click **"Save preferences"**
